INDIAN FESTIVALS

Across the globe, Hindus celebrate a diverse number of festivals and celebrations. The festivals typically celebrate events from ancient Indian and often coinciding with seasonal changes. There are many festivals which are primarily celebrated by specific sects or in certain regions of the Indian subcontinent.

The festive season is widely celebrated. These events have a historic significance to them and are rooted in various cultural and linguistic norms. The onset of the famous Diwali festival marks the beginning of lighting 'diyas' (earthen lamps), beautifying homes, offering gifts and visiting friends and family. Similarly, the Holi festival is described as the 'Festival of Colors', with its magnificent rain dance and the 'gulaal'.
